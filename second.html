Planet Profiles

Explore the planets of our solar system and learn how they compare to Earth.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and has extreme temperature fluctuations.

Compared to Earth: Mercury is much smaller, with no moons.

Fun Fact: A day on Mercury is longer than its year!

Venus

Venus is Earth's "sister planet" with a runaway greenhouse effect and extreme heat.

Compared to Earth: Similar size, but uninhabitable due to heat and pressure.

Fun Fact: Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life, with a breathable atmosphere and water.

Compared to Other Planets: Earth is uniquely suited for life.

Fun Fact: Earth is the only planet with plate tectonics.

Mars

Mars, the "Red Planet," has the largest volcano and deepest canyon in the solar system.

Compared to Earth: Mars is smaller and colder, with a thin atmosphere.

Fun Fact: Mars has two moons, Phobos and Deimos.

Jupiter

Jupiter, the largest planet, is famous for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm.

Compared to Earth: Jupiter is 11 times larger and has 79 moons.

Fun Fact: The Great Red Spot could fit three Earths inside it!

Saturn

Saturn is known for its stunning ring system made of ice, rock, and dust particles.

Compared to Earth: Saturn's diameter is 9 times larger, but it's less dense.

Fun Fact: Saturn has 82 confirmed moons!

Uranus

Uranus rotates on its side, giving it extreme seasons. Its blue-green color comes from methane.

Compared to Earth: Uranus is 4 times larger and extremely cold.

Fun Fact: Uranus has 27 known moons.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet, known for its vivid blue color and fierce winds.

Compared to Earth: Neptune is 4 times larger and extremely stormy.

Fun Fact: Neptune was discovered through mathematical predictions.
